About

I am a PhD candidate in Earth Sciences at The Ohio State University studying submarine landslides, sediment transport, and marine geohazards. My research integrates sedimentology, geomechanics, and machine learning to understand mass transport deposits and slope stability in convergent margin systems including the Japan Trench.

GitHub Google Scholar ORCID LinkedIn

Research

My work focuses on probabilistic modeling of submarine slope failure, sediment routing, and disturbance classification in marine sediment cores. I investigate how seismic forcing, pore pressure, and sediment properties influence slope stability and the formation of mass transport deposits.
